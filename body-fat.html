US Navy Body Fat Method

The US Navy method uses body circumference measurements to estimate body fat percentage. It was developed by Hodgdon and Beckett at the Naval Health Research Center.

Male (inches): BF% = 86.010 × log₁₀(waist − neck) − 70.041 × log₁₀(height) + 36.76

Female (inches): BF% = 163.205 × log₁₀(waist + hip − neck) − 97.684 × log₁₀(height) − 78.387

How to Measure Correctly

  • Height: Stand straight without shoes, measure to the top of your head.
  • Neck: Measure just below the larynx (Adam's apple), sloping downward to the front.
  • Waist (Male): Measure at the navel level.
  • Waist (Female): Measure at the narrowest point, usually above the navel.
  • Hips (Female): Measure at the widest point below the waist.

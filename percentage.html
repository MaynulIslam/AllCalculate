What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" comes from the Latin per centum, meaning "by the hundred." Percentages are used everywhere — from calculating discounts and tax to understanding statistics and grades.

Common Percentage Formulas

What is X% of Y?
Result = (X / 100) × Y

X is what % of Y?
Percentage = (X / Y) × 100

Percentage Change
Change % = ((New − Old) / |Old|) × 100

Increase by %
New Value = Original × (1 + %/100)

Decrease by %
New Value = Original × (1 − %/100)

Real-World Examples

  • Discounts: A $80 jacket is 25% off → 25% of 80 = $20 saved → you pay $60
  • Sales Tax: A $50 item with 8% tax → 8% of 50 = $4 → total $54
  • Tip Calculation: 20% tip on a $65 bill → 20% of 65 = $13 tip
  • Grade Score: You got 42 out of 50 → 42 is 84% of 50
  • Pay Raise: Salary of $50,000 with a 5% raise → new salary is $52,500
  • Stock Change: Stock went from $120 to $150 → that's a 25% increase

How to Quickly Estimate Percentages in Your Head

  • 10%: Move the decimal point one place left (10% of 250 = 25)
  • 5%: Half of 10% (5% of 250 = 12.5)
  • 20%: Double 10% (20% of 250 = 50)
  • 25%: Divide by 4 (25% of 200 = 50)
  • 50%: Divide by 2 (50% of 180 = 90)
  • 1%: Move the decimal two places left (1% of 3,500 = 35)

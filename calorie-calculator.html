How to Calculate Daily Calories

Your daily calorie needs are calculated using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, the most accurate formula for estimating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) for most people.

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation:
Male BMR = (10 × weight_kg) + (6.25 × height_cm) − (5 × age) + 5
Female BMR = (10 × weight_kg) + (6.25 × height_cm) − (5 × age) − 161

TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier

What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body burns at complete rest — just to maintain vital functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production.

What is TDEE?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is your maintenance calorie level — the number of calories you need to eat to keep your current weight, accounting for your physical activity.

Calorie Deficits & Surpluses

One pound of body fat equals approximately 3,500 calories. To lose 1 lb per week, you need a daily deficit of 500 calories. Deficits below 1,200 calories/day (women) or 1,500 (men) are generally not recommended without medical supervision.

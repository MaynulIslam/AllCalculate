How to Calculate Square Footage

Square footage (sq ft) is the most common unit for measuring area in the US. It's used for rooms, houses, yards, and any other two-dimensional space.

Formulas by Shape

Rectangle / Square: Area = Length × Width
Circle: Area = π × radius²
Triangle: Area = ½ × base × height
L-Shape: Area = Rectangle A + Rectangle B

Converting Between Units

Results are always shown in square feet plus the equivalent in other common units:

  • 1 sq ft = 144 sq in
  • 1 sq yd = 9 sq ft
  • 1 sq m = 10.7639 sq ft
  • 1 acre = 43,560 sq ft

Waste Factor (Why It Matters)

When ordering flooring, tile, or carpet, always add extra for cuts, waste, and future repairs. Industry standard recommendations:

  • Hardwood / Laminate (straight lay): Add 10%
  • Hardwood (diagonal lay): Add 15%
  • Tile (straight pattern): Add 10%
  • Tile (diagonal pattern): Add 15%
  • Carpet: Add 10%
  • Paint: One gallon covers ~400 sq ft per coat

Tips for Measuring a Room

  • Measure the longest length and widest width of the room
  • For irregular rooms, divide them into rectangles and add the areas
  • Don't subtract closets — most flooring is sold by total sq ft
  • Measure twice, order once!

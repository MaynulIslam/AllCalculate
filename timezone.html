Understanding Time Zones

The world is divided into 24 primary time zones, each roughly 15 degrees of longitude wide, corresponding to one hour of time difference. However, political and geographic boundaries mean many real-world zones deviate significantly from this pattern.

UTC (Coordinated Universal Time) is the primary time standard. All other time zones are expressed as UTC offsets (e.g., UTC+5:30 for India).

DST (Daylight Saving Time) shifts clocks forward by 1 hour in spring and back in autumn in participating regions.

Common Time Zone Abbreviations

  • UTC/GMT: Universal Coordinated Time — the baseline reference
  • EST/EDT: Eastern Standard/Daylight Time (UTC-5 / UTC-4)
  • CST/CDT: Central Standard/Daylight Time (UTC-6 / UTC-5)
  • MST/MDT: Mountain Standard/Daylight Time (UTC-7 / UTC-6)
  • PST/PDT: Pacific Standard/Daylight Time (UTC-8 / UTC-7)
  • IST: India Standard Time (UTC+5:30, no DST)
  • JST: Japan Standard Time (UTC+9, no DST)
  • AEST/AEDT: Australian Eastern Standard/Daylight Time

Tips for International Meetings

When scheduling across time zones, always confirm whether DST applies to both locations. Spring and autumn transitions often fall on different dates in different countries, causing temporary shifts in the offset between two zones.

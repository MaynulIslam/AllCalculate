GPA Scale Explained

GPA (Grade Point Average) is a standard way to measure academic achievement. Most US colleges use a 4.0 scale.

Semester GPA vs. Cumulative GPA

Semester GPA is calculated only from courses in a single term. Cumulative GPA accounts for all courses taken across all semesters. To calculate cumulative GPA, use the "Previous GPA" fields above to include past semesters.

GPA Formula

GPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Credit Hours Attempted

Quality Points for a course = Grade Points × Credit Hours
Example: A (4.0) in a 3-credit course = 12 quality points

How GPA Affects Scholarships and Admissions

  • Dean's List: Typically requires a 3.5+ GPA per semester
  • Magna Cum Laude: Usually 3.7–3.89 cumulative GPA
  • Summa Cum Laude: Typically 3.9–4.0 cumulative GPA
  • Academic Probation: Often triggered by a GPA below 2.0
  • Graduate School: Most programs require a 3.0+ undergraduate GPA
  • Merit Scholarships: Many require maintaining a 3.0–3.5 GPA

Tips for Improving Your GPA

  • Retake courses in which you earned a D or F — many schools allow grade replacement
  • Focus on high-credit courses — improving a 4-credit course grade has more impact than a 1-credit course
  • Take advantage of pass/fail options for challenging electives
  • Visit office hours and form study groups early in the semester
  • Don't overload your schedule — a lighter load with better grades can yield a higher GPA

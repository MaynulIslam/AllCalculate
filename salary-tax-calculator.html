How This Calculator Works

This calculator estimates your take-home pay after federal income tax, provincial/state income tax, and mandatory payroll deductions. Rates are based on 2025 tax brackets.

🍁 Canada – What's Deducted

  • Federal income tax: Five brackets from 14.5% to 33%, with the Basic Personal Amount (BPA) of $16,129 reducing your tax via a non-refundable credit.
  • Provincial/territorial income tax: Each province has its own brackets and BPA on top of federal tax.
  • CPP (Canada Pension Plan): 5.95% on earnings between $3,500 and $71,300 (YMPE). An enhanced CPP2 applies at 4% on earnings $71,301–$81,200.
  • EI (Employment Insurance): 1.64% on insurable earnings up to $65,700. Quebec residents pay a reduced rate of 1.31% due to the provincial QPIP plan.
  • Quebec note: Quebec residents receive a 16.5% federal tax abatement, significantly reducing their federal portion.

🇺🇸 USA – What's Deducted

  • Federal income tax: Seven brackets from 10% to 37%. The standard deduction for single filers is $15,000 in 2025.
  • State income tax: Varies widely — 9 states have no income tax (AK, FL, NV, NH, SD, TN, TX, WA, WY), others range from 3% flat to nearly 14% progressive.
  • Social Security (FICA): 6.2% on wages up to $176,100 in 2025.
  • Medicare: 1.45% on all wages, plus an additional 0.9% on wages above $200,000.

Disclaimer

This calculator provides estimates only and does not account for RRSP/401(k) contributions, tax credits beyond the basic personal amount, investment income, self-employment taxes, local/city taxes, or other personal deductions. For exact figures, consult a tax professional or use the official CRA/IRS calculators.
